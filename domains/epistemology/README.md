# Epistemology Skills for AI Agents

This directory contains a comprehensive collection of epistemology skills designed specifically for AI agents. These skills provide frameworks for managing knowledge, beliefs, evidence, and reasoning processes in complex information environments.

## Generated Skills

### 1. SKILL.evidence_weighting.md
**Purpose**: Evidence weighting systems for AI agents to evaluate, prioritize, and integrate multiple sources of information with varying reliability, confidence levels, and contextual relevance.

**Key Capabilities**:
- Evidence quality assessment and scoring
- Confidence calibration and adjustment
- Source credibility modeling with temporal decay
- Conflict resolution using probabilistic reasoning
- Multi-agent evidence aggregation
- Uncertainty quantification and management

### 2. SKILL.belief_revision.md
**Purpose**: Belief revision systems for AI agents to dynamically update, maintain, and reorganize their knowledge base in response to new evidence and changing conditions.

**Key Capabilities**:
- Belief consistency checking and maintenance
- Revision strategy selection based on importance and evidence
- Cognitive dissonance resolution
- Temporal belief evolution tracking
- Multi-agent belief coordination
- Belief impact assessment

### 3. SKILL.source_reliability.md
**Purpose**: Source reliability assessment systems for evaluating, tracking, and managing the credibility of information sources across multiple dimensions.

**Key Capabilities**:
- Historical accuracy analysis and trend detection
- Bias detection and quantification
- Methodological rigor assessment
- Temporal reliability tracking with decay mechanisms
- Contextual appropriateness evaluation
- Multi-source credibility aggregation

### 4. SKILL.hypothesis_competition.md
**Purpose**: Hypothesis competition systems for managing, evaluating, and resolving competing explanations and theories in complex problem-solving scenarios.

**Key Capabilities**:
- Automated hypothesis generation and diversity
- Competitive evaluation using multiple criteria
- Evidence-based ranking and selection
- Falsification strategy design
- Multi-agent hypothesis coordination
- Uncertainty quantification for hypothesis confidence

### 5. SKILL.epistemic_guardrails.md
**Purpose**: Epistemic guardrails systems to ensure epistemic safety, prevent knowledge corruption, and maintain reasoning integrity.

**Key Capabilities**:
- Epistemic boundary definition and enforcement
- Knowledge validation protocols
- Reasoning constraint enforcement
- Epistemic risk assessment and mitigation
- Knowledge corruption prevention
- Continuous epistemic monitoring and auditing
